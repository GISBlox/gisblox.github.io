# Playgrounds (EN)

A _playground_ is your experimental space inside MCP Studio. It’s a safe, isolated environment where you can combine datasets, parameters, and geometries without affecting production. Playgrounds are built for iteration, insight, and creativity.

## Why Playgrounds?

Playgrounds help you:

- test ideas without risk
- tweak parameters and see results instantly
- build complex spatial logic in small, understandable steps
- share insights with colleagues without exposing full projects

## How a Playground Works

A playground consists of three core elements:

### 1. Inputs

Datasets, parameters, filters, or geometries. Each block is modular and reusable.

### 2. Actions

Spatial or logical operations such as intersections, buffers, aggregations, calculations, or validations.

### 3. Results

Maps, tables, statistics, exports, or workflow outputs.

## Best Practices

- Start small: one dataset, one action, one result.
- Use clear names for blocks and actions.
- Keep variables close to where they’re used.
- Share playgrounds to accelerate team insight.

## Example

A simple playground for identifying _risk areas_:

1. Input: buildings + flood zones
2. Action: intersect buildings with zones
3. Result: list of buildings in risk area + map visualization
